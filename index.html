Feed it sound

Four ways to drive the visuals.

Pick a source on the start screen and the fractals lock onto the beat — the zoom speeds up with the bass and surges on every kick.

The visuals

Five fractal worlds, endlessly zooming.

Each style zooms inward forever and is rendered live on the GPU. They're “made of slime” — a wet glossy sheen plus jelly physics that squash and wobble on every kick.

Auto-cycle

Projection mapping

Fit the image to any wall, booth or object.

Press M and four corner handles appear — drag each one until the visuals line up exactly with your surface, perfect for angled or keystoned projectors. Your mapping is saved automatically for next time.

Prefer Resolume or MadMapper? Run it fullscreen and capture the window as a source — or skip all that and use the built-in corner mapping.

Under the hood

One file. No libraries. No backend.

WebGL shaders

Every frame is a GLSL fragment shader running on the GPU — that's how the fractals stay smooth while zooming forever.

Web Audio API

An FFT analyser splits the sound into frequency bands so bass, kick and highs each drive different parts of the motion.

Self-contained

The whole visualizer is a single hand-written HTML file — no frameworks, no build step, nothing to install.

Private by default

Music and microphone are processed locally on your device. Nothing is ever uploaded or recorded.
